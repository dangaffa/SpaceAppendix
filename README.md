# SpaceAppendix
Appendix of various space terms



## Orbital Mechanics

**GEO**: GEostationary Orbit, an orbit high enough that it's orbital period is the same as the body it's orbiting, meaning it's over the same point on the surface at any given time

**GTO**: Geostationary Transfer Orbit, an orbit designed to get from LEO to GEO, often used by launch vehicles in order to save their own fuel, allowing for the spacecraft to enter GEO itself

**LEO**: Low Earth Orbit

**L1/2/3/4/5**: Lagrange points, Lagrange points are points of stability between two objects. When otherwise unspecified, it's most commonly refering to Sun-Earth lagrange points

**SOI**: Sphere Of Influence, area where one body's gravity is the strongest compared to any other surrounding it. 

**SSO**: Sun Synchronous Orbit: A type of polar orbit which means that the satellite always passes over any given point on the surface at the same solar time

**TLI**: Trans Lunar Injection, the rocket manuver that sends a spacecraft from an Earth orbit into an orbit taking it into the Moon's SOI

**TMI**: Trans Mars Injection, the rocket manuver that sends a spacecraft from an Earth orbit into an orbit taking it into Mars' SOI

## Basics

**CFM** Cryogenic Fuel Management, technologies designed to support very cold fuels in space

**FSS**: Fixed Service Structure, a tower designed to access payloads of a rocket that stayes in place, as opposed to a Rotating Service Structure

**GSE**: Ground Support Equipment, the parts of the launch system that stay on the ground, "Stage Zero" as SpaceX would put it

**LBO**: Low BoilOff, technologies which allow for little fuel to be lost in CFM

**LC**: Launch Complex, prefix for launchpads which are operated by NASA

**LV**: Launch Vehicle, another term for rocket

**RBO**: Reduced BoilOff, see LBO

**RSS**: Rotating Service Structure, a tower designed to access payloads of a rocket which rotates

**SLC**: Space Launch Compled, prefix for launchpads which are operated by the US Space Force

**SRB**: Solid Rocket Booster, side-rockets which use solid fuel rather than the more common liquid fuel of rocket engines

**SRM**: Solid Rocket Motor, any rocket engine which uses solid fuel rather than the more common liquid fuel

**SSTO**: Single Stage To Orbit, often describes a launch vehicle that, unlike most launch vehicles, is able to make orbit without separating into multiple stages

**TVC**: Thrust Vector Control, moving the engine or it's bell to change the direction of thrust, allowing rotation of the rocket

**ZBO**: Zero BoilOff, technologies which allow for no fuel to be lost in CFM

## Organizations/Companies

**CASC**: Chinese Aerospace Science and technology Corporation, China's nationalized launch vehicle manufacturer

**CNES**: Centre National d'Études Spatiales (literal translation: National Centre for Space Studies): French space administration

**CNSA**: Chinese National Space Administration, China's overarching space administration

**CSA**(*ASC in french*): Canadian Space Agency

**DLR**: Deutsches zentrum für Luft- und Raumfahrt (literal translation: German center for Air and Spaceflight): German space administration

**ESA**: European Space Agency, Europe's primary overarching space agency

**JAXA**: Japan Aerospace eXploration Agency: Japan's space administration

**LM**: Lockheed Martin, aerospace firm best known for the Orion capsule and the Atlas V rocket

**MDD**: McDonnel Douglass, defunct aerospace corporation. Best known for the DC-X and the Delta II rockets, it would be merged into Boeing in 1997

**NASA**: National Aeronautics and Space Administration

**NSF**: NASA SpaceFlight: news organization dedicated to covering space, also runs the app/website Next SpaceFlight

**UKSA**: United Kingdom Space Agency

**USAF**: United States Air Force

**USSC**: United States Space Command

**USSF**: United States Space Force

## Programs

**CLD** Commercial LEO Destinations: NASA program for commercial space stations

**CLPS**: Commercial Lunar Payload Services, unmanned lunar landing program by NASA

**EELV**: Evolved Expendable Launch Vehicle, program designed to select the next generation of launch vehicles for the US Air Force

**NSSL**: National Security Space Launch, evolution of EELV

**SDI**: Strategic Defense Initiative, nicknamed "Starwars," a weapons research and development project started under Reagan designed to create, among other things, space based weapons of mass destruction

## Launch Vehicles

**BFR**: Big ~~Fucking~~ Falcon Rocket, old name for SpaceX Starship

**BONG**: Blue Origin New Glenn

**DIV/DIVH**: Delta IV(4) and Delta IV Heavy

**F9/FH**: Falcon 9/Falcon Heavy, SpaceX's current launch vehicles

**ITS**: Interplanetary Transport System, old name for SpaceX Starship

**NLS**: National Launch System, series of shuttle derived rockets proposed under Bush in 1991

**SDHLV**: Shuttle Derived Heavy Launch Vehicle, a large SDLV, also used to describe a 2009 proposal for a side mounted SDLV with an upper stage designed to send Orion and a lunar lander to the moon, as well as cargo to LEO

**SDLV**: Shuttle Derived Launch Vehicle, a rocket with design heritage from the Space Shuttle

**SLS**: Space Launch System, NASA rocket which is the backbone of the Artemis program

**STS**: Space Transportation System, alternate name for the NASA Space Shuttle, also used as a prefix for each Shuttle mission

## Others

**ABC**: Artemis Base Camp, Artemis Program's lunar base

**BOLE**: Booster Obsolescence and Life Extension, often used when discussing the boosters designed for SLS Block 2

**BMM1/BMM2**: Blue Moon Mark 1/2, Blue Origin lunar landers, Mk1 is uncrewed, Mk2 is crewed

**CEV**: Crew Exploration Vehicle, original name for the Orion capsule

**CLT**: CisLunar Transporter, Lockheed Martin manufactured refueling system for BMM2

**CPS**: Cryogenic Propulsion Stage, small second stage designed for long-term flights under the Constellation program, also used in early SLS concepts

**CSM**: Command and Service Module, Apollo's crew capusle and propulsion unit

**CV**: Centaur V (either V or 5), the variant of the Centaur upper stage used on the Vulcan rocket, which is also planned to be used on evolved variants of the SLS

**EDS**: Earth Departure Stage, upper stage designed for the Ares V launch vehicle, also used on early SLS concepts

**EUS**: Exploration Upper Stage, (former) evolved upper stage for the SLS

**HLS**: Human Landing System, the first Artemis-era lunar lander, often referring to just the Starship derived Starship HLS

**ICPS**: Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage, upper stage for SLS designed for 3 flights, derived from the DIV upper stage

**LM**: Lunar Module, lunar lander for the Apollo program

**SLD**: Sustainable Lunar Development, name for the porgram making the second generation lunar landers for Artemis, applicable to the BMM2 and evolutions of Starship HLS

**SPS**: Space Power Stations, large solar panels which beam power down to Earth

**SPS**: Space Propulsion System, engine for the Apollo CSM

**SRMU**: Solid Rocket Motor Upgrade, 2nd generation SRBs designed for the Titan IV launch vehicle

**SSME**: Space Shuttle Main Engine, liquid hydrogen engine used to power the Space Shuttle as well as the SLS

**STME**: Space Transportation Main Engine, engine derived from the SSME, designed for NLS
